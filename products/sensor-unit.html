Sensor Unit for PV Module


Overview

Module-level monitoring provides the most detailed and precise information on solar power system performance, it can help identify module problems such as partial shadings or blown diodes. With such solution, O&M teams are able to save their fault-finding time and increase maintenance efficiency.

The sensor unit is the core component of the NxSensor system, it is a SoC (System-on-Chip) device that can be integrated into the junction box of a PV module, continuously measuring the voltage and temperature data at module level.

Besides monitoring, the sensor unit also supports the functionality of module-level shutdown, complying with NEC 2017 690.12 RSD requirements.

NxSensor sensor unit device

Tech Specs

  • Voltage measurement from 7V to 60V, with ±0.2V accuracy;
  • Temperature measurement from -25°C to 75°C, with ±0.5°C accuracy;
  • PCB dimension of 40 mm * 28 mm * 4 mm;
  • NEC 690.12 rapid shutdown compliant;
  • Built-in 600V surge protection;
  • 2.4GHz ISM radio band and wireless connectivity in HOHWave™ network;

Features

  • Enable remote monitoring of PV module, fault alerts, rapid shutdown protection;
  • Wireless connectivity supported, no hard wires needed for data communication;
  • Compact size, could be embedded into the double-glass module's junction box;
  • Easy installation and easy configuration for existing solar power system;

Installation on Existing Junction Box

Some junction box's back cover could be easily opened, and the junction box may have enough internal space for a NxSensor sensor unit. The sensor unit has two thimbles for voltage data sampling, they are supposed to stick onto the positive and negative points of the junction box, respectively. Since the positive and negative points may have different distance according to different junction boxes, please consult NxSensor for the right product model that fits your condition.

Please note this installation method is only for professionals to install few sensor units for validation purpose, and always be particularly careful in modifying the junction box or making connections at the solar modules.
